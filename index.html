Taekwondo Korean martial art

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art characterized by its emphasis on kicking techniques, which are often dynamic and powerful. It’s both a combat sport and a form of self-defense. Taekwondo practitioners also train in various hand techniques, forms (patterns of movements), and sparring drills.

The word “taekwondo” is derived from the Korean words“tae” (meaning foot),“kwon” (meaning fist), and“do” (meaning way of). So, Taekwondo translates to“the way of the foot and fist.”

Taekwondo became popular internationally in the 20th century, particularly after it was included in the Olympic Games as a demonstration sport in 1988 and as an official sport in 2000. It’s known for its high kicks and fast-paced movements. Training in Taekwondo typically involves forms (poomsae), sparring (kyorugi), self-defense techniques, and breaking techniques (kyukpa).
